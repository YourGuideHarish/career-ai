Part 02 — The Formula

Every Great Bullet
Follows a Pattern

You don't need to memorise examples. Just follow this structure and fill it in.

The CAR Formula
[Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [How/Tool] + [Result/Impact with Number]
Example: "Streamlined [what] requirement intake using JIRA [how], reducing ambiguity-related rework by 20% [result]."
The XYZ Formula (Google Recruiter Method)
Accomplished [X], as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]
Example: "Improved feature adoption by 18% [X], measured via in-product tracking [Y], by running structured A/B experiments across 3 user segments [Z]."

Types of Numbers You Can Use

📊

Percentages

Show relative improvement — great for efficiency, adoption, error reduction.

+18% adoption, −20% rework, 90% adherence
🔢

Absolute Counts

Show scale of work — users impacted, items handled, clients onboarded.

500+ users, 6 clients, 60+ backlog items
⏱️

Time & Frequency

Show speed or consistency — how fast, how often, over what period.

3 months, 8 sprints, weekly cadence
💰

Revenue / Cost

Show business value — revenue generated, costs saved, budgets managed.

₹2.5L revenue, $50K cost saved
👥

Team / Stakeholders

Show complexity of coordination — how many people, geographies, teams.

30+ stakeholders, 2 countries, 5 teams
🎯

Rankings / Ratings

Show standing — top performer, NPS scores, review ratings.

Top 10% cohort, 4.8/5 CSAT, Rank 2
Part 03 — The Hard Part

"But I Don't Know
My Numbers."

This is what everyone says. Here are 6 practical approaches to find or estimate them — honestly.

📬

Mine Old Emails & Reports

Go back to your sent folder, sprint reviews, status updates, and QBRs. You've written your numbers already — you just haven't collected them. Search for words like "improved", "reduced", "completed", "shipped".

→ Found a stakeholder email saying "sprint delivered on time for 4th week in a row"? That's a number.
🗓️

Ask Your Manager / Team Lead

Your manager tracks impact you may not have visibility into. A simple message — "Hey, do you recall the impact of the X project I led?" — can surface data you'd never find alone. Most are happy to share.

→ "The campaign you ran contributed to a 12% lift in Q3 signups" — that's a resume bullet.
📐

Estimate with Ranges

You don't need exact numbers. Use honest ranges or approximations. "Reduced X by ~15–20%" or "Impacted 200+ users" is credible. The key is your estimate should be conservative and defensible in an interview.

→ If you saved ~2 hours/week for a 10-person team, that's ~1,000 hours/year saved — use it.
🔗

Look at Tool Dashboards

JIRA, Confluence, Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Tableau, Salesforce — all of these log your activity and output. Pull your own ticket history, sprint velocity reports, or campaign dashboards and extract data.

→ JIRA shows you closed 140 tickets in 6 months with an avg resolution time of 2 days? That's usable.
🧮

Reverse-Engineer from Context

If you know the size of the audience or system you touched, you can work backwards. "Our platform had 10K DAU, my feature was shown to 30% of them" = you impacted 3,000 users. Do the math and be upfront about it.

→ "Feature rolled out to 30% of 10K users = ~3,000 users impacted in the pilot."
🤝

Use Benchmark Data

If your individual numbers aren't available, use industry/team benchmarks as context. "Our team's sprint adherence improved from ~60% to 90%+ after I introduced structured backlog reviews" — even if that came from a team retro, it's valid.

→ Industry avg NPS for SaaS is 30. If yours was 45, say: "Maintained NPS 50% above industry benchmark."
Questions to Ask Yourself for Every Role
If you can answer even half of these, you'll have enough to quantify your bullets.
How many people / users / clients did this affect?
How long did it take vs. how long it would have taken before?
What was the team size I worked with or led?
How many times did this repeat (weekly, monthly)?
What metric went up or down because of my work?
What was the before vs. after state?
How much revenue, cost, or time was at stake?
How many items, features, or deliverables were involved?
What was the success rate / completion rate / adherence %?
What was the geography or scope (cities, countries, teams)?
Part 04 — Watch Out

Common Mistakes
Even With Numbers

Numbers help, but only when done right. Here's what trips people up.

  • 1
    Don't invent numbers. Recruiters ask about them in interviews. If you can't recall or recreate the logic behind a number, don't put it. Use ranges or qualitative language instead — "significantly", "measurably" are okay if you have nothing else.
  • 2
    Don't quantify activity, quantify outcome. "Sent 200 emails" is activity. "Ran a 200-touch outreach campaign that converted at 12%, generating 24 qualified leads" is outcome. Always ask: so what?
  • 3
    Don't stuff every bullet with numbers. 2–3 numbers per bullet is usually the max before it starts reading like a spreadsheet. Pick the most impactful metric and frame around it.
  • 4
    Don't use irrelevant vanity metrics. "Our team had 50 people" tells nothing about your contribution. Be specific to your own impact, not your company's size or team's headcount unless it shows complexity you managed.
  • 5
    Don't forget to attribute clearly. If a result was a team effort, say "contributed to" or "supported X% improvement" rather than claiming sole ownership. Integrity matters — and interviewers probe this.
  • 6
    Don't lead with the number. Lead with the action, then support it with data. "Reduced churn by 15% by implementing weekly NPS reviews" > "15% churn reduction via weekly NPS reviews." Verbs show agency.
Quick Reference

The Cheat Sheet

Tear this off. Use it every time you write or update a resume bullet.

📋 Resume Numbers Cheat Sheet
One question for every weak bullet: "Compared to what? For how many people? Over how long?"
✍️
Start bullets with a strong action verb: Led, Built, Reduced, Launched, Improved, Streamlined, Partnered, Drove
🔢
Add at least one number: %, count, duration, frequency, ranking, revenue, or team size
🎯
Connect effort to outcome: "by doing X" → "resulting in Y" — the causal link is the whole point
📊
Use ranges if uncertain: "~15–20%" or "50+" are honest and still impactful
📅
Add time context when it shows speed or consistency: "within 3 months", "across 8 sprints"
🚫
Delete any bullet that a robot (or anyone else) could have written for your job — those are job descriptions, not achievements
👂
Read each bullet out loud. If it sounds like something you'd write on a form, rewrite it
💬
For every bullet, ask: "Can I defend this number in a 30-second interview answer?" If yes, keep it.
